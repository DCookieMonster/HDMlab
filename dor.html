I have showed that the extent to which groups converge to correct solutions to problems can be linked to the computational complexity class of the problem. My educational background and experience reflects my research interests in the computational and social sciences. Since the first year of my undergraduate studies I have been working in the Data Science Lab at BGU, headed by my advisor Dr. Kobi Gal. My initial position was a full stack developer and programmer, but my responsibilities and research interests have evolved considerably since that time, I am now running my own research program in the lab, including programming online experiments on Amazon Turk. I am highly skilled in programming, specifically Python, Java, JavaScript, HTML, PHP as well as online web development.

I am currently working with Dr. Levi Leis from Federal University of Viçosa on helping planners to solve hard problems. We are going to use people’s estimations of cost as a heuristic to facilitate classical planning algorithms.

We are using the "Floor Tiles" problem as our pilot. This problem with large grid has considered a hard problem to solvers, we hope that by using people we will be able to estimate more accurate the states.
